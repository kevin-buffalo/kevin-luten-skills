# Voice Profile Document Template

Read this at Step 8 (Assemble and deliver). Build the user's document to this structure. The document has two layers serving two readers: a human-readable profile (so the user can sanity-check and maintain it) and an AI-facing directive block (so a future-chat AI can act on it directly).

Adapt the template to the person — don't include empty sections. Every trait must be specific and evidence-based; cut anything generic.

## Structure

```markdown
# My Writing Voice — [Context Label]

> Attach or paste this at the start of a chat (or add it as a project/reference file) when you want the AI to write in your voice.
> This describes HOW I write, not how to structure what I'm writing. Apply the voice; do not add hooks, frameworks, or structural moves I didn't ask for.

## Profile — for the human (reference, not instructions)

This section is here so *you* can read, sanity-check, and update your profile over time. The AI writing for you should treat it as background; the operative instructions are in the Directives section below.

A few sentences in plain language describing the overall feel of this voice — what someone would notice first. Then the specific components, each concrete and evidence-based:

- **Sentence rhythm:** [specific — avg length, variation, fragment use]
- **Punctuation:** [specific habits and avoidances]
- **Vocabulary & register:** [level, concrete/abstract, characteristic words]
- **Words I reach for / never use:** [the signatures and the allergies — *my* words, drawn from my samples, not a generic list]
- **Formality:** [where on the axis; how it shifts by audience if relevant]
- **Directness vs. hedging:** [specific]
- **Person & stance:** [specific]
- **Figurative language:** [whether and what kind]
- **Tics & signatures:** [the idiosyncratic moves]

## How I write — examples in my own words

[2–5 short real excerpts from the user's own samples, each framed by what it shows. E.g.:
- *"[real sentence]"* — how I open something plainly, no warm-up.
- *"[real sentence]"* — my habit of breaking to a short line for emphasis.
Omit this whole section if the document will be shared rather than kept personal.]

## Directives — for the AI writing in my voice (this is the operative section)

When writing as me, follow these. They are the instructions to act on; the profile above is background. Keep each concrete enough to follow without guessing:

- Sentences: [e.g., "Average 12–18 words. Break to a 3–5 word sentence for emphasis 2–3 times per piece. Fragments are fine."]
- Punctuation: [e.g., "Use em dashes sparingly — one or two per piece, never two in one sentence. No semicolons."]
- Diction: [the person's actual word habits and *their own* allergies, drawn from their samples — e.g., "Plain, concrete verbs. I never say 'folks' or 'super excited.'" Do NOT insert a generic list of AI-cliché words here; this is about how *this person* writes, not about avoiding chatbot tells.]
- Contractions: [always / never / when]
- Formality: [specific register instruction]
- Stance: [e.g., "State things directly. Don't hedge with 'it seems' or 'perhaps.'"]
- [any other directive that captures a distinctive trait]

**Apply voice only.** Do not impose structure (opening hooks, persuasion frameworks, CTA placement, listicle formatting). Match how I sound, whatever I'm writing.

**Don't caricature.** A habit I use occasionally should stay occasional. Don't take one trait and turn it into a tic that shows up in every sentence — that produces an impression of me, not my actual voice.

**Check before finishing.** After drafting, re-read once and ask: does this actually sound like the person in this profile? If a line rings false, fix it.
